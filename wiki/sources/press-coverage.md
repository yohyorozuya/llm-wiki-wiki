# Press & analysis coverage (VentureBeat, Atlan, Denser.ai)

## Overview

The mainstream and analyst coverage that shaped public reception of the [[llm-wiki-pattern]] — led by VentureBeat's April 3 piece, which drove the "bypasses RAG" framing. Ingesting it confirms the ~400,000-word scale anchor, adds the Lex Fridman "ephemeral wiki" idea, and surfaces two new critics.

## What it adds

- The [[ephemeral-wiki]] concept (Lex Fridman, via VentureBeat): generating a temporary focused mini-knowledge-base for a single task, e.g. voice-mode on a run.
- Two sharper critics (@gnusupport, @LudoE11) with a distinct "category error" angle — see [[category-error-critique]].
- Confirms the "knowledge as compiled code" framing as the most influential ([[compilation-vs-retrieval]]).

## A flagged error in the wild

Some low-quality posts conflate the pattern with Karpathy's unrelated **llm.c** project and call it on-device RAG. This is false; recorded so the wiki does not propagate it. The [[llm-wiki-pattern]] is an idea-file, not built on llm.c, and is explicitly *not* query-time RAG.
