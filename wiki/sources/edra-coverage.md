# Edra funding coverage (TechCrunch, Tech.eu, The Recursive)

## Overview

March 2026 coverage of [[edra]]'s $30M stealth exit. Confirms the founders, the round, and the product — and corrects one framing: Edra is a *parallel* enterprise product, not a build on the [[llm-wiki-pattern]].

## What it confirms / corrects

Founders Eugen Alpeza (CEO) and Yannis Karamanlakis (CTO), ex-Palantir Forward Deployed AI Engineering (London); $30M total, ~$23.8M Series A led by Sequoia's Luciana Lixandru (board seat), with 8VC, A*, HubSpot Ventures. Product: "Living Playbooks" — reverse-engineering how a business runs from operational data (tickets, emails, logs) into executable knowledge. Early customers: HubSpot, ASOS, Cushman & Wakefield, easyJet.

Important correction: the round closed in **March 2026, before Karpathy's April gist**. Edra is conceptually parallel (operational data → living knowledge base) but **not derived from** the [[llm-wiki-pattern]]. The [[research-report]]'s "enterprise version of the LLM Wiki" is an analogy, not a lineage.
