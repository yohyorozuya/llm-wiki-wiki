# Edra

## Overview

A New York/London startup pursuing the same *problem* as the [[llm-wiki-pattern]] at enterprise scale, founded by former Palantir Forward Deployed AI Engineering leaders Eugen Alpeza (CEO) and Yannis Karamanlakis (CTO). It builds "Living Playbooks" — reverse-engineering how a business actually runs from operational data (support tickets, emails, logs, chat) into a continuously-updated, executable knowledge base [[edra-coverage]]. Emerged from stealth March 2026 with $30M total (~$23.8M Series A led by Sequoia's Luciana Lixandru, who took a board seat; plus 8VC, A*, HubSpot Ventures). Early customers: HubSpot, ASOS, Cushman & Wakefield, easyJet.

**Important:** Edra is *parallel*, not derived. Its round closed in March 2026, before Karpathy's April gist. The [[research-report]]'s "enterprise version of the LLM Wiki" is an analogy — Edra independently arrived at the same core insight (Alpeza: "The hardest part of automation was never the AI. It was capturing the knowledge").
