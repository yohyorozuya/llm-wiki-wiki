# The "Category Error" Critique

## Overview

A critique attributed to gist commenters @gnusupport and @LudoE11 (reported by Denser.ai, [[press-coverage]]), judged "sharp and largely fair." Three linked claims: the [[llm-wiki-pattern]] "collapses past ~1,000 files"; "LLM-only maintenance leads to compounding hallucinations"; and calling a folder of AI-written Markdown a "wiki" is a **category error**, since a real wiki is defined by human collaboration and no humans collaborate here.

## Relationship to other critiques

The scaling claim overlaps with the report's ~1000-page threshold (see [[llm-wiki-vs-rag]]); the compounding-hallucinations claim is the [[lossy-summarization-critique]]; but the "category error" point is distinct and more philosophical — it questions whether the *naming* smuggles in connotations (collaborative, authoritative) the artifact hasn't earned.
