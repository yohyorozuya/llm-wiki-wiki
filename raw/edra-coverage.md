# Edra funding coverage (TechCrunch, Tech.eu, The Recursive, etc.)

Sources: TechCrunch, Tech.eu, The Recursive, BEAMSTART, AI Market Watch, NewDecoded. March 2026. Fetched late May 2026.

## The round

Edra emerged from stealth ~March 18-19, 2026 with a $30M total / ~$23.8M Series A led by Sequoia (partner Luciana Lixandru, who joined the board), plus 8VC, A* (Kevin Hartz's fund), and HubSpot Ventures. An earlier ~$6.5M seed was co-led by 8VC and A*. New York + London based.

## Founders

Eugen Alpeza (CEO, Croatian) and Yannis Karamanlakis (CTO, Greek). Met at university 13 years ago; spent years at Palantir. Alpeza built out major commercial accounts and led the launch of Palantir's AI Platform; Karamanlakis was Palantir's first Forward Deployed AI Engineer, focused on getting models out of demos into production. They started Palantir's Forward Deployed AI Engineering in the London office.

## What Edra does

Builds AI agents that "learn how a business operates, then automate the work." Connects to existing systems (ServiceNow, Zendesk, Outlook) and "reverse-engineers how a business actually runs from existing systems" — analyzing support tickets, emails, logs, chat histories — to create "executable knowledge: structured, white-box instructions that agents can act on." The artifact is a "Living Playbook": a continuously-updated, executable knowledge base that "evolves as the business changes," vs static documentation.

Founding insight (Alpeza): "The hardest part of automation was never the AI. It was capturing the knowledge." Real processes live in people's heads, not docs ("tribal knowledge"). Early adopters: HubSpot, ASOS, Cushman & Wakefield, easyJet. Initial focus: IT service management + customer support; Lixandru frames a horizontal-platform opportunity beyond ITSM.

## Relationship to the LLM Wiki

Edra is a separate, prior, enterprise product — it predates and is independent of Karpathy's gist (the round closed March, the gist published April). It is conceptually parallel (turn operational data into a living, executable knowledge base) but NOT built on or derived from the LLM Wiki pattern. The report's framing of Edra as "the enterprise version" is an analogy, not a lineage.
